# Rolling Resistance: Why Flat Tires Make Cars Harder to Push

When you try to push a car with a flat tire, it feels much harder than pushing a car with properly inflated tires. The reason lies in a fundamental concept of mechanical engineering called **rolling resistance**.

Rolling resistance is the **force that opposes motion when a wheel or tire rolls over a surface**.  
- A perfectly rigid wheel on a hard surface would have almost no rolling resistance.  
- Real-world tires (made of rubber) deform as they roll, which consumes energy.

When a tire is **underinflated or flat**, it deforms much more against the road. This increased deformation causes higher **hysteresis losses** (energy lost as heat), which directly increases rolling resistance.
